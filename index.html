Objectives

Why ...

Unifcation and standardization problems.

At I2CVB, we like to challenge the impossible along with the status quo therefore we strive to settle a multi-skilled community pursuing common goals to achieve excellence through collaborative continuous improvement.

At I2CVB, we believe that Free Software and Quality Management have already reshaped the world and that is time to apply some of the successful practices learned in such domains to expand the boundaries of research in computer vision and specially for the medical imaging case.

What ...

Open data; evaluation methods; comparison framework; reporting platform.

The lack of common data combined with non-aggregated assessing strategies result in non-existent or misleading comparisons, which difficult to acknowledge relevant novel methodologies. A common goal for the research and development communities is to overcome such limitations, which can be successfully addressed by co-creation and collaboration.

I2CVB aims at serving as foundation for collecting and sharing data as well as providing common evaluation methodologies. Furthermore, a fair comparison is only achieved by using common data and evaluation techniques.

Prostate

Motivations

Prostate cancer (CaP) has been reported on a worldwide scale to be the second most frequently diagnosed cancer of men accounting for 13.6% (Ferlay et al. (2010)). Statistically, in 2008, the number of new diagnosed cases was estimated to be 899, 000 with no less than 258, 100 deaths (Ferlay et al. (2010)).

Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) provides imaging techniques allowing to diagnose and localize CaP. The I2CVB provides a multi-parametric MRI dataset to help at the development of computer-aided detection and daignosis (CAD) system.

Datasets

Overview:

In order to provide a wide dataset, the multi-parametric data have been acquired from two different commercial scanners:

  • A 1.5 Tesla General Electric (GE) scanner.
  • A 3.0 Tesla Siemens scanner.

Each subsect is composed of a total of 30 patients:

  • Twenty patients with biopsy proven CaP.
  • Ten "healthy" patients with negative biopsy results.

Modalities:

The I2CVB provides a multi-parametric MRI dataset. The modalities available the different dataset are:

  • T2-Weighted (T2-W) MRI.
  • Dynamic Contrast Enhanced (DCE) MRI.
  • Diffusion Weighted Imaging (DWI) MRI.
  • Magnetic Resonance Spectroscopic Imaging (MRSI).

Image format:

Regarding some technical aspects of the dataset, the T2-W MRI, DCE MRI and DWI MRI will be delivered in DICOM format.

Regarding the MRSI data will be either provided in RDA (Siemens) or DICOM (GE) format.

Finally all the ground-truth images for each modalities will be provided in DICOM format.

Ground-truth:

For each modality, a set of ground-truth is provided. The ground-truth is composed of four different classes: (i) prostate gland, (ii) peripheral zone (PZ), (iii) central gland (CG), (iv) CaP.

Still in deployement ...

We are still working on the annotation of the different dataset.
